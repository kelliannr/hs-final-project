Places to See

Matsushiro Samurai School

The Matsushiro Samurai School was built in 1853 by Sanada Yukinori, who was the 9th leader of the Matsushiro Clan. The Sanada family placed great importance in educating samurai and their children, in both literary (reading, writing and arithmetic) and military (martial arts, tactics, etc.) which led to the building of this school. The school disbanded during the Meiji Restoration (1868) and the facility is used as Matsushiro Elementary. It was designated as a national historical site in 1953, it was the only school in Japan that retains its original form and structure. Click the image to the right for more information.

Matsumoto Castle

Matsumoto Castle is the oldest existing castle in Japan and recognized as a national treasure. The castle features moon viewing pavilion which indicates the lifestyle of old lords. Located between the castle and Matsumoto station, are two shopping streets named Nawate-dori, which is lined with small speciality shops and eateries and Nakamachi (shopping district) that is lined with picturesque Kura (earthen-walled storehouse) -type buildings. Suwa Grand Shrine

The Suwa Grand Shrine is the head shrine of over 10,000 suwa shrines spread across Japan and beyond. The shrine is center on Lake Suwa and is made up of 4 complexes. The south side of the lake house the two Upper Shrines and north side house the two Lower Shrines. The Upper Shrines has the main Hon-Miya, which is the main shrine, and the simpler, original Mae-Miya, which is the beginning or head shrine. At the Lower Shrines, the vernal Haru-Miya features the whimsical Manji stone statue, and the autumnal Aki-Miya , which is the heart of Shimosuwa-juku, a post town along the Nakasendo Road.
