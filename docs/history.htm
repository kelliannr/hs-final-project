History

The Nagano Prefecture is located in the former Shinano Province and has developed from the Nara period. Nagano city was formerly known as Zenkõji city and is the capital of the prefecture. The city grew up around the Zenkō Temple, which was founded in the 7th century. Nagano later developed as a market town and post station along the Hokkoku Road. It is now an important commercial center with food-processing, electrical machinery, and printing and publishing industries. Nagano is also a cultural and educational center and is the site of Shinshū University. In 1876, Chikuma and Nagano merged to form the Nagano Prefecture. The high speed transportation network was improved with the opening of Hokuriku Super Express system and the completion of the expressways. Nagano city hosted the 1998 Winter Olympic Games .
