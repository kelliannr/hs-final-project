Nagano's Yuru-chara

What is Yuru-chara?

Yuru-chara are the characters that are instantly recognized as a form of a region’s special product, culture , history or other outstanding feature, and they’re considered familiar figures and widely loved. Whenever these characters appear live (with people wearing full-size character costumes to animate them) at tourist sites or special events, they’re instantly surrounded by crowds surging forward and exclaiming “kawaii!” (how cute!) and vying to have their pictures taken with the characters. The mascot characters also decorate a wide range of products for sale, from stationery items to souvenir cookies, which make popular gifts.

Arukuma

Arukuma is a green bear that wanders around, fitting as his name means “walking bear,” who collects hats. Each hat he collects represents one of Nagano’s specialities like apples, chestnuts, persimmons, mushrooms, lettuce, soba and wine. He was created to represent Nagano’s apple orchids, since he wears an apple hat most of the time.
